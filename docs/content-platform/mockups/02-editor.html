Why pairing matters

Some plants improve each other's growth, deter pests, or share space efficiently. Others compete for the same nutrients.

  • Tomatoes + basil — basil repels common tomato pests.
  • Carrots + onions — onions mask the carrot-fly scent.
  • Beans + corn — beans fix nitrogen the corn needs.
Keep fennel away from almost everything — it inhibits nearby growth.

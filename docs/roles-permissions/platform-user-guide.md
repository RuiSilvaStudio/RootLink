# Your RootLink Account: Roles, Verification & What You Can Do

This page explains how accounts work on RootLink — what kind of account you have, what you can do at each stage, how to build trust on the platform, and what happens if something goes wrong. If you've ever wondered "why can't I do that yet?" or "what does 'verified' mean?", this is the page for you.

---

## What kind of account do I have?

When you sign up, you pick the account type that matches you:

- **Individual** — this is you, a regular community member. If none of the other types below fit, this is your default.
- **Professional** — you offer professional services through the community (consulting, trades, expertise, whatever your field is). If you're not offering a professional service, sign up as an Individual instead.
- **Organization** — you're signing up on behalf of a group: a company, non-profit, association, or similar. Organizations manage their own team of members (more on that below).
- **Partner** — your business has a formal partnership with RootLink itself (for example, a law firm or accounting firm that supports the platform). This isn't a self-serve signup type; it's set up as part of a partnership relationship.
- **Supplier** — similar to Partner, but for businesses that supply services to RootLink itself (hosting, infrastructure, and so on).
- **Platform** — this is RootLink's own staff account type. You won't sign up as this — it exists so the people who run the platform can step in when needed, across every other account type.

Whichever type you choose (except Platform), you start at the same place: a brand-new, unverified account.

---

## What can I do at each level?

Everyone starts small and grows into more capability as they stick around and build trust. Here's the journey:

**Visitor** — this is you the moment you register, before you've confirmed your email. You can browse and read public content, but you can't post, comment, or interact yet.

**Persona** — once you verify your email, you become a Persona. Now you can submit links, follow other members and organizations, send direct messages, join groups and RSVP to events, and leave comments.

**Contributor** — active, trusted members can become Contributors. This is where things open up: you can write and edit your own articles, create and edit groups, list products, run your own events (with sponsors and vendors attached), and offer courses.

**Moderator** — some communities have Moderators, who do everything a Contributor can, plus help keep things running smoothly: reviewing and approving other members' submitted articles, and stepping in to remove a comment that breaks the rules.

**Admin** — Admins have broader oversight over their community's accountability — for example, sending notifications to everyone in the community, and handling password resets for other members if needed.

**Super Admin** — the top level within a given community (an organization has its own; individuals and professionals rely on RootLink staff instead — see below). A Super Admin can edit or archive anything belonging to that community, and grant or remove trusted-publisher status for members.

A few things worth knowing about how this ladder works in practice:

- **How high you can go depends on your account type.** Individuals can rise as far as Contributor. Professionals can rise as far as Admin. Organizations are the only account type with their own Super Admin — because they're managing their own team, not just their own content.
- **Nobody can promote someone to their own level or higher** — only to a level below them. And every promotion or demotion is reviewed and logged, not an instant, unchecked toggle.
- **Nobody approves their own work** — even a Super Admin can't approve their own article submission. Approval always comes from someone else.

## How do I become verified / trusted?

RootLink uses a few different "trust markers" so the community knows who they're dealing with:

- **Verified user** — you get this simply by verifying your email as a Persona, by being referred by someone already trusted, or by being added directly by a verified organization.
- **Verified professional** — if you're signed up as a Professional, you additionally need a valid national tax/business registration ID and an activity registration number, on top of a verified email. This confirms you're a real, registered professional.
- **Verified organization** — a manually-checked badge confirming an organization is a genuine, registered entity. Over time this will involve uploading real documentation for a human to check, not just a self-declaration.
- **Trusted user** — this is earned, not given. You need at least three published articles with no negative reviews, and you need to explicitly agree to take responsibility for what you publish (the "Publisher Responsibility" agreement). Once both are true, you get **self-publish**: your future articles skip the review queue and go live right away.

Trust is genuinely earned, and it can be taken away — if one of your qualifying articles later gets retracted or found in violation of the rules, your trusted status is automatically re-checked, not left alone until someone happens to notice.

## What happens if I break the rules?

Two levels, both separate from your role — a brand-new member and a long-time Admin are held to the same standard:

- **Suspended** — this is temporary and has a set end date. While suspended, you can still read content, but you can't post, comment, or rate anything. It lifts automatically once the time is up — you don't need to do anything.
- **Banned** — this is indefinite and more serious: you're fully locked out, including reading, and anything you've published is taken down while the ban is active.

One important detail: any special badges you've earned (like trusted publisher) are put on hold while you're suspended or banned — they don't quietly keep working in the background. And if you had content queued up to auto-publish because you're a trusted publisher, it won't auto-publish once you're back — it goes through a fresh review first.

## I run an organization — what's different for me?

Organizations work a bit differently from individual accounts:

- **You have your own Super Admin**, unlike individual or professional accounts. Whoever completes your organization's registration and verification automatically becomes that first Super Admin — no waiting on approval, since there'd be nobody else yet to approve it.
- **You manage your own team.** Adding members, promoting trusted people within your organization, and moderating your own community's content is handled inside your organization, not by RootLink staff — though certain platform-wide actions (like archiving a group with attendees from other organizations, or permanently dissolving the organization) always require RootLink staff to sign off, since those can affect people outside your organization too.
- **Converting into an organization is one-way.** If a professional account converts into an organization, they become that organization's first Super Admin, but they start at the bottom of the new organization's ladder in terms of rank — and any trust badges they'd built up don't carry over automatically. Content they've already published stays theirs either way.
- **Closing an organization is a real, considered action, not a quick delete.** It requires sign-off from RootLink staff. When it happens, members become regular individual accounts (their organization rank/badges don't carry over), and the organization's content is archived — hidden, not deleted — with a 30-day window to reverse the decision before it becomes permanent.
- **If your organization has a presence inside someone else's content** — say, listed as a sponsor on another organization's event — and your organization is later banned or closed, that specific listing is automatically hidden too. It never touches the host's own content, just your organization's piece of it, and it's reversible in that same 30-day window if things get sorted out.

**If you're a Partner or Supplier** (rather than a full Organization): your account type doesn't have its own Admin or Super Admin tier — extra permissions are granted individually by RootLink staff as needed. If more than one person is associated with your partner/supplier account, one of you can be named the **primary contact**, who can add or remove people from your team roster. The primary contact can't grant themselves or others extra permissions, though — that part still goes through RootLink staff.

## Can someone else manage things on my behalf?

Yes — this is called **delegation**. An organization's Super Admin (or, platform-wide, RootLink's own leadership) can hand off a specific job to someone they trust, without making that person a full Admin or Super Admin.

For example, a Super Admin could delegate "manage any event in the organization" to a trusted Moderator, so that person can help run events across the whole organization — without also giving them the ability to suspend members or promote/demote people. Some especially sensitive actions (suspending/banning someone, or promoting/demoting people) are never delegable — those always stay with the Super Admin directly, so there's always a clear, accountable person behind them.

If the person holding a delegated permission is later demoted, suspended, banned, or leaves the organization, that delegated permission is automatically taken back — nobody has to remember to clean it up.

## Glossary — plain terms vs. the technical spec

If you ever want to cross-reference the technical roles & permissions specification, here's how the plain-language terms above map to it:

|---|---|

| Regular verified member | Persona (rank 1) |
| Member who can create their own content | Contributor (rank 2) |
| Community helper who reviews others' content | Moderator (rank 3) |
| Community manager with broader oversight | Admin (rank 4) |
| Top authority within your community | Super Admin (rank 5) |
| RootLink's own staff | Platform entity |
| Confirmed real registered professional | Verified professional |
| Confirmed real registered organization | Verified organization / practitioner badge |
| Earned self-publish privilege | Trusted user / trusted publisher badge |
